<h2>Cyclistic Case Study</h2>

R Programming Google Sheets Google Slides

Acting as a Junior Data Analyst for the marketing team at Cyclistic, a Chicago-based bike-share company, I was tasked with analyzing user data to identifiy trends. The Director of Marketing established a clear business goal: the future success of the company depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships.

Cyclistic offers three pricing tiers: Single-ride passes, Full-day passes, and Annual memberships. Customers using single-ride or full-day passes are classified as "Casual Riders", while those with annual subscriptions are "Cyclistic Members." The objective of this analysis is to understand how these two groups use bike differently to drive conversion strategies.

For this analysis, I used R for data inspection and sampling, Spreadsheets for data cleaning and visualization, and Slides for the presentation. Due to the high volume of trip data, I applied sampling techniques to create a manageable yet representative dataset. Using a sample size calculator, I extracted the dataset with 98% confidence level and a 2% margin of error.

View Sheets and PPT here!

Code in R

Gathered data and determined the sample size for each month in year 2022 using R. Used 98% Confidence level and 2% Margin of Error when sampling the population.

Data Cleaning and Manipulation in Sheets

Performed data cleaning by removing duplicates, blanks, and incorrect formats in Sheets. Also manipulated the data by creating new columns for ride_length and day_of_week.

Pivot Tables

Combined monthly data by season and created pivot tables for: (1) average ride length of casual riders and members, (2) average daily ride length of casual riders and members, and (3) ride count per day of the week.

Presentation for Stakeholders

Presented the problem, discussed the data, and provided insights and recommendations for action through data-drive decision-making.
